The basics of diamond grading

The basics of diamond quality grading involve an understanding of the universally accepted standard for assessing the quality of diamonds. They comprise the 4 Cs; namely Cut, Color, Clarity and Carat Weight. Each of these four parameters plays paramount role in determining the precise quality of a diamond.

While the cut and the symmetry associated with it exhibit the overall brilliance, the color adds to the intensity of the beauty of the diamond. Clarity stands for the flawlessness of the piece, and carat weight is an obvious reference to the weight. The IFtoSI diamond buying guide presents an in-depth description of each of these four attributes along with charts respective to the grades and illustrates the differences among the grades.
