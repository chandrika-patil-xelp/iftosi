Once cut and polished, all diamonds possess a shared set of characteristics, often referred to as the anatomy of the diamond. While the individual proportions, angles and placement of these common characteristics vary for diamonds of different shapes, their definition is the same.

Crown
The entire portion of the diamond that sits above the girdle. The table as well as the star, bezel , and upper girdle facets are all part of the crown. usually constitutes the bulk of a diamonds weight.
Table
The uppermost , flat surface of a diamond which runs parallel to the plane of the girdle.
Star Facets
The facets that sit adjacent to the table, responsible for directing light that enters the diamond down to the pavilion and lower girdle facets.
Bezel Facets
The kite shaped facets that sit between the table and girdle, resposible for directing light that enters the diamond down to the pavilion and lower girdle facets.
Upper Girdle Facets
The lowest facets on the crown, sitting just above the girdle. Responsible for directing light that enters the diamond down to the pavilion and lower girdle facets.
Girdle
The outer edge of the diamond , where the crown meets the pavilion . the girdle may be faceted or smooth.
Pavilion
The entire portion of the diamond that sits below the girdle. The lower girdle facets,pavilion facets , and culet are all part of the pavalion. usually constitutes the bulk of a diamonds weight.
Lower Girdle Facets
The highest facets on the pavilion , sitting just below the girdle . Responsible fo redirectiong light that enters the diamond back out through the crown.
Pavilion Facets
The facets that sit adjacent to the culet, responsible for redirecting light that enters the diamond back out through the crown.
Culet
The culet is the small area at the bottom of the pavilion.The culet can be a point, or a very small facet sitting parallel to the table.
Length
When viewed from above as a diamond would sit in a ring , length is the diameter measured from the "top" to "bottom".Stated in millimeters . Length is always the first of three numbers shown under "measurements" for each diamond.
Depth
The distance from the table to the culet when the diamond is viewed from the side.State
Width
When viewed from above as a diamond would sit in a ring, width is the diameter measured from "side" to "side". Width is always the measure perpendicular to length. Stated in millimeters . Width is always the measure perpendicular to length. Stated in millimeters . Width is always the second of the three numbers shown under "measurements" for each diamond.
Depth %
Calculated by dividing the dpth by the width. If a diamond is 3mm in depth , and has a midth of 4mm, the depth % is 75. Depth % is a measure of cut . The more shallow the cut of a diamond , teh higher the depth %.
Length / Width Ratio
Calculated by dividing the length by the width.A perfectly round or square diamond has a L/W ratio of 1.00(since it's length and width are equal).
Table %
Calculated by dividing the width of the table by the width of the diamond. If the table is 3mm wide , and the diamond is 4 mm in width , the table %is 75. The ;arger the table in relation to the diamond , the higher the table %.
Girdle Width
A measure of the thickness of the diamond's girdle.The measure is taken only at the "valleys" of the girdle, never the peaks. Usually stated in terms of a nine point scale , from the Extremely Thin through Extremely Thick. If the girdle valleys vary in width, two values are given, one of the widest and one for the narrowest points.
Crown Angle
Refers to the number of degrees between the plane of the table and bezel facets. The angle of the crown facets will determine how much light is reflected into the pavilion at an angle that will allow it to be reflected back up through the crown (where the observer cn perceive it).
Culet Size
The culet can vary in size from a single point , to a small facet . Culet size is usually expressed using a seven point scale , from None(a point) to Very Large. If the culet is too large, it might appear as an inclusion when the diamond is viewed from above.
Pavilion Angle
Refers to the number of degrees between the planes of the table , and the pavilion facets. the angle determines how much of the light which has travelled into the lower part of the diamond will be successfully reflected back through the crown (where the observer can perceive it).
