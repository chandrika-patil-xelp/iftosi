Diamond Color

As we begin our discussion on diamond color, let us remember that the stone at its best is colorless. Diamonds are rendered colorful by the crystals growing inside and the due to structure distortion the stones undergo upon pressure application. Diamonds with no color are the rarest and the costliest. However, diamond is available in every color of the rainbow and the ones considered desirable range from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow). Note that colored diamonds are graded using a different color scale than the one used to determine white diamond.

Multiple factors impact how a diamond looks to its buyer. These factors include, among others, its environment, the mounting of the stone and carat weight. In a smaller diamond color is difficult to perceive. However, with the increase in carat weight color in the diamond becomes increasingly prominent.

Most gemologists use the color scale grade of Colorless, Near Colorless, Faint Color, Very Light Color and Light Color to determine color in a diamond. Presented below is brief understanding of each of these:

gia diamond color scale
Colorless

Diamonds in this grade are considered to be the best. However, colorless diamonds are extremely rare and, hence, highly expensive. Within the range of D to Z, diamonds falling into the categories of D, E and F are viewed to be Colorless. While it is important to note that there are subtle differences in color among these three, it requires trained eyes to spot the differences even when these diamonds are kept side by side.

Near Colorless

Diamonds with very little color in them fall within the category of Near Colorless. These diamonds are distinctive as they appear colorless when mounted in jewelry. Diamonds falling into the categories between G to J are Near Colorless while prices of each individual grades increases by 15-20 percent beginning with G. Due to the extreme rarity of the Colorless grade, Near Colorless diamonds attract premium price and common in markets.

Faint Color

Diamonds with these three color grades namely, K, L and M belong to this grade. Relatively easy to detect with naked eye, a jewelry mounted Faint Color diamond weighing less than 0.50 carat will appear colorless while color could be spotted in larger Faint Color diamond.

Very Light Color

N-R color range diamonds exhibit noticeable color, are not expensive and are not sought after while few jewellers stock them.

Light Color

S-Z diamonds fall within this grade and they exhibit highly discernible color and are seldom preferred by consumers.

While the discussion above serves as general guidelines for diamond color, it is depended upon individual preference of consumers. Overall, it can be put forth that despite the Colorless diamonds being viewed as the best grade, consumers tend to favor the diamonds with slight tinge of yellow. This preference can be attributed to the warmth of the slightly yellow diamond and the rarity and expensiveness of the Colorless diamonds.
