Hearts And Arrows

Hearts and Arrows in diamond design and cut refers to a particular pattern involving perfect symmetry and angles. Effects such achieved in combination with several factors including pattern positioning, shape and spacing contribute to determining a diamond’s visual appeal and, hence, the pricing. When a diamond cutter uses certain specifications during his artistry, the hearts and arrows pattern are visible upon using special magnification and specific viewer tools; for example, the H&A Viewer.

Modern, portable H&A viewers

The hearts and arrows pattern has gained prominence over the years to become one of the most desirable characteristics in the recent times. Consumers and diamond evaluators worldwide have attached so magnified importance to the pattern that a cut with optimal symmetry in the hearts and arrows pattern is often viewed as a testimony of perfection while cutters too have sacrificed the overall cut quality to achieve the pattern.

However, we at IFtoSI believe that this should be viewed as an opinion blurred by magnified marketing of the importance of the pattern. While the presence of the hearts and arrows in diamonds definitely adds to the overall brilliance of the stone, the pattern should not be weighed too much to determine the quality and value of a diamond.
