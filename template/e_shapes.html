Shapes

Each diamond has its own characteristics which determine the shape of the stone. Consumers’ knowledge about diamond shapes goes a long way in helping to choose the best diamond to suit one’s choice. The round cut shape has long been preferred. However, with the advancement of technology enabling evolved options consumers have started opt of varied shapes which include pear shaped, cushion and radiant cut diamonds, among several others.

While a thorough knowledge of each of the available shapes is not mandatory for individual buyers, basic information about each shape helps in choosing the perfect diamond to your personality. We have presented below an introduction to all the popular shapes.

As already stated, the round cut diamond is the most preferred and sought after diamond shape. The shape also represents more than one third of all diamonds sold in the jewellery market. Alongside the precise calculations used in its cut, the flexibility it offers in balancing color, clarity and cut grades renders it superior to usually available fancy shapes. Round cut diamonds achieve optimal brilliance due to good polish and symmetry allowing adequate light reflection which in turn maximizes its brightness.
Named after the shape of the fruit, the unique pear-shaped diamond comes in excellent symmetry. It is an enviable combination of the marquise and the round shape. Presenting a pointed tip on one end – which should be pointing towards the wearer – the elongated shape combined with the length of the diamond gives a slimming effect on the fingers. The length-to- width ratio of a pear-shaped diamond should ideally be between 1.45 and 1.75. While cut, color and clarity of the pear-shaped diamond should mostly depend on the wearer’s individual preference, it is important to go for the ones which are technically flawless and aesthetically appealing.
Among all diamond shapes heart shaped diamonds offer the distinctive choice and the cut is one of the most complex to create requiring high level of dexterity. While traditional heart-shaped diamond should have length-to- width ratios between 0.90 and 1.10, consumers are recommended to select a diamond larger than 0.5 carats, as finer details won’t be emphatically visible in smaller stones. Consumers preferring smaller diamond should opt for a bezel or 3-prong setting as this will better display the unique shape of the cut.
One of the most popular among all square shapes, princess cut has existed since 1979. Prior to achieving its present shape and name, the cut design had gone through several years of optical research involving names such as Quadrillion Cut and Barion Cut. It comes in pyramidal shape comprising either 57 or 76 facets. Princess shape diamonds are ideal for long fingers and are equally suitable for solitaire engagement rings and earrings.
Rectangular in shape, the emerald-shaped diamonds got their name from the shape which was used for the cutting of emeralds. Emeralds come with truncated corners and rows of step-facets while the top is flat. The unique cuts in the diamond are achieved from the pavilion; however, they are not comparable to brilliant-cut and have less fire. When selecting an emerald cut diamonds buyers should look for clarity grade of SI 1 or higher. In cases where consumers prefer emeralds below this grade, we recommend reviewing the clarity grade on the diamond certificate. Also, buyers of emerald-shaped should ideally look for a length-to- with ratio between 1.25 and 1.5. Ideal for budget-conscious consumers, Emerald cut diamonds look great in rings especially on longer fingers.
Consumers who prefer square emerald cut over rectangular shape in diamond should consider asscher shaped diamonds. Introduced by Joseph Asscher in 1902, asscher shaped diamonds became highly popular in 1920s and then started regaining popularity exactly after a century of their introduction. Being square in shape the ideal length-to- width ratio would naturally have to be 1.00, but consumers can also buy between length-to- width ratio of 1.00 and 1.05. Although modern cutting techniques have continued to optimize the excellence of the asscher cut diamond, timeless look continues to dominate buyers taste.
Marquise shaped diamonds maximize carat weight and presents a narrow and larger look. It bears some similarity with the Oval shaped diamonds in the sense that it accentuates slender fingers. Rumored to be developed for King Louis XIV to match the smile of Marquise de Pompadour, marquise shaped diamonds have striking appeal.  While a traditional marquise diamond length-to- width ratios stand between 1.75 and 2.25, individual preference determines the narrowness a marquise diamond.
Oval shaped diamonds are comparable to round shaped diamonds as far overall brilliance is concerned. The modern oval cut as it exists today came into being in 1960 while diamonds bearing oval shaped name had existed for more than 200 years earlier.  Characterized by symmetrical, elongated rounded shape, oval shaped diamonds optimizes carat weight, elongates shorter fingers and are ideal for accentuating slender fingers.
Radiant diamonds were introduced to the industry in the 1970s as an alternative to the emerald cut. True to their name, radiant diamonds exude vibrant, memorable glow. They come in square, near- square, or rectangular shape and therefore are great reflectors of light. Trimmed corner squares of the radiant are the signature of this diamond, and they connect the cushion and the princess cut. Radiant diamonds come with an intricate 70 facet cut. Consumers who prefer the fire of round diamonds and the superiority of asscher or emerald diamonds will find the radiant diamonds to be a great combination of both. If you are looking for a square radiant cut diamond, go for length-to- width ratios between 1 and 1.05. Buyers who like a rectangular shape should opt for length-to- width ratios greater than 1.10.
Cushion-shaped diamond stones round shape corners with bigger facets which contribute to increasing their brilliance. Being large, the facets exhibit the diamond’s brilliance. Available in shapes ranging from square to rectangular, cushion-cut diamond shapes have evolved over the years. However, buyers seem to prefer the ones that bear a combination antique design and modern performance. While cut in cushion diamonds are matter of personal preference, the ones that are usually in high demand come in rectangle of 1.10-1.20 length to width. For color, the ones ranging between the grades of D-F are preferred.
