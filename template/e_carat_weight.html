Diamond Carat weight

Carat refers to the globally accepted unit of weight measurement of gems and diamonds. However, one should be careful not to confuse Carat with “karat” which refers to the purity of gold. Further, caution should be exercised to understand that carat weight do not reflect the size of a diamond.

As per accepted international standard, one diamond carat equals 200 milligrams or 0.200 grams or one- fifth of a gram. The carat weight measurement is again subdivided into 100 points (one point is equal to 0.01 carat) or described in 1/4 increments by jewellers.

Among the 4Cs of the diamond, carat weight is the most objective parameter and significantly impacts the pricing, alongside the size, the shape and cut of a stone. Despite larger diamonds being a rarity, the carat weight should not be the sole determinant to buy the stone. Buy your diamond for its beauty and consistency rather than the weight.

While carat weight does not represent the size of a diamond, the size itself is determined by taking two other criteria into consideration. They are: The millimeter distance on the top of the diamond and the stone’s cut grade.

Few things to remember

There are a few fundamental factors to consider while buying your diamond:

1.

The heavier the carat weight, the more expensive the stone becomes. Price does not in proportion to the increase in carat weight. A single heavier diamond will cost more than two smaller diamonds weight amounting to the single diamond.

2.

Half a carat is NOT half the size of one carat. It is actually half the weight of one carat.

3.

Always consider a wearer’s body part specifications while buying diamond. For example, consider the size and shape of wearer’s finger when buying a diamond for a ring. A stone will look larger on longer and slimmer fingers while the same piece of diamond will appear smaller if the wearer has shorter and wider fingers.

4.

Setting of the diamond enhances or reduces the look of the diamond. When put in slim settings, a diamond stone looks prominent but the same diamond when put in a solid setting will tend to look smaller.

5.

Two diamonds of same weight may not be equal in their value. The one with seamless Cut, Color and Clarity will attract premium price while inappropriate Cut, Color and Clarity will only to negatively impact its brilliance and thereby, its value.
