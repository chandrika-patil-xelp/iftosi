Diamond Gridle

The girdle of a diamond refers to the edge at the outermost of it. Girdle divides the crown of a diamond from its pavilion and in a polished it is the widest portion visible when viewed in its setting. Diamond girdle can be polished, faceted or rough. However, these aspects hardly influence the value or the look of the diamond.

What determine the value of a diamond are its girdle thickness ratings. The girdle width can be described as discussed in the below parameters:

very Thin

Extremely Thin
Extreme thin girdle diamonds should be avoided as they would fragile and consequently prone to breaking. Further, extreme thickness allows room for mistake as they call for extra carefulness during the setting process of the stone. (Commonly used acronyms are ETN, ETH, XTN, EXN).
Very Thin
These also call for caution during stone setting and their durability will remain in doubt due to sharp edges. Similar to Extremely Thin girdle, Very Thin diamond girdles are best avoided. (Commonly used acronyms are VTN, VT, VETN).
Thin-Medium
Girdles falling under this range of width are considered to be of ideal proportion.
Slightly Thick, Thick
Girdles of this range of width belong to the excellent to ideal proportion.
Very Thick
Diamonds with this range of girdle with are considered to be of good proportion.(Commonly used acronyms are VTK, VTH).
Extremely Thick
Too thick girdles make a diamond appear smaller and are middle-heavy, making a diamond weigh more. (Commonly used acronyms are ET, EXTK, XT, XTK).

We at IFtoSI recommend buying a diamond with a girdle width ranging between Thin and Thick.
