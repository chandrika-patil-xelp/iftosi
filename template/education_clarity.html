Diamond Clarity

Clarity in a diamond grading, and therefore its value, attains importance due to the factor of rarity attached to higher clarity in a diamond. Put simply, clarity refers to the transparency despite the presence of microscopic inclusions and blemishes, which are kind of inherent in diamonds due to the situation under which they are formed and due to the process they undergo to attain brilliance.

Minor flaws on a diamond’s surface borne out of the polishing process are referred to as blemishes; those inside the stone formed naturally during its birth are called inclusions. While inclusions and blemishes are integral part of a diamond’s clarity, significantly high presence of these in a diamond impacts the way light passes through it, and thereby its visible brilliance.

The grading a diamond’s clarity in contingent upon multiple factors including the stone’s perceptible imperfections under 10x magnification, its color, number, size, and reflectivity. World over, clarity of diamonds are graded by using the 11 point scale. The scales can be explained as below:

Below is the GIA diamond clarity chart with definitions, accompanied by further explanatory comments from IFtoSI:

GIA Diamond Clarity Scale
FL
Flawless
FL rated diamonds are free from all blemishes or inclusions even upon using 10x magnification. Flawless diamonds are extremely rare.
IF
Internally Flawless
In IF rated diamonds only minor blemishes are visible to a skilled grader using 10x magnification while inclusions are not visible. FL and IF diamonds look very similar and skilled grader requires 10x magnification to discern differences.
VVS1 VVS2
Very, Very Slightly Included
VVS1 and VVS2 are superior diamonds and it is very difficult to see imperfections under 10x magnification.
VS1 VS2
Very Slightly Included
In VS1 and VS2 rated diamonds inclusions are not detectable by the naked eye while minor inclusions are easily noticeable under 10x magnification.
SI1 SI2
Slightly Included
In SI1 rated diamonds imperfections are not visible to the naked eye while in SI2 diamonds imperfections require close inspection to be exposed by the onlooker.
L1
Included
In L1 rated diamonds imperfections are always evident to even the unaided eye.
I2-I3
Diamonds
12-13 rated diamonds have low durability due to too many imperfections.

There are multiple factors which graders consider before determining a diamond’s clarity grade. They are size, nature, number, location, and relief. Size in clarity grade refers to the inclusions size which significantly impacts a diamond’s value. Nature refers to the superficiality or depth of inclusions while greater number in clarity grade signifies lower grade in a diamond.

Location refers to the position of the inclusion/blemish in the diamond. Imperfections near the center of the table significantly impact clarity while imperfections near the girdle points toward future damage risk. Relief is the clarity of the inclusion.
