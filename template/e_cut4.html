Diamond Symmetry

Symmetry in a diamond points to the precision involved in the cut design of the stone facets. Contrary to popular belief inconsistent symmetry does not represent poor cutting design. The cutter here introduces willful imperfections to optimize the weight of the stone as minimal variance in weight influences a diamond’s perceived quality value and the actual price. For example, a diamond with “Good” symmetry and weighing 1 carat will be more sought after than a piece with “Excellent” symmetry but weighing, say 0.97 weight.

Symmetry grade in a diamond falls in to the following parameters:

Excellent
Symmetry imperfections are not noticeable at 10x magnification.
Very Good
Symmetry imperfections are very hard to trace at 10x magnification.
Good
It is difficult to point out symmetry imperfections at 10x magnification.
Fair
Imperfections visible at 10x magnification.
Poor
Defects are obvious even to the untrained eye.

Like all other grading parameters, symmetry should not be the sole deciding factor when buying a diamond from your preferred jeweler. A good looking diamond is a combination of excellent polish and excellent symmetry. However, a diamond does not automatically become desirable only with a perfect combination of these two; a poor Cut design will impact the value and appearance of the diamond. On the other hand, excellent Cut design in combination of good symmetry and good polish will makes a diamond desirable.
